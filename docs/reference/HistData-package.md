# Data sets from the History of Statistics and Data Visualization

The HistData package provides a collection of data sets that are
interesting and important in the history of statistics and data
visualization. The goal of the package is to make these available, both
for instructional use and for historical research.

Some of the data sets have examples which reproduce an historical graph
or analysis. These are meant mainly as starters for more extensive
re-analysis or graphical elaboration. Some of these present graphical
challenges to reproduce in R.

They are part of a program of research called *statistical
historiography*, meaning the use of statistical methods to study
problems and questions in the history of statistics and graphics. A main
aspect of this is the increased understanding of historical problems in
science and data analysis trough the process of trying to reproduce a
graph or analysis using modern methods. I call this "Re-visioning",
meaning to *see again, hopefully in a new light*.

A number of these are illustrated in our book, Friendly & Wainer (2021),
*A History of Data Visualization and Graphic Communication*, and some
are re-produced in R in the companion web site,
<https://friendly.github.io/HistDataVis/>.

## Details

Descriptions of each DataSet can be found using `help(DataSet)`;
`example(DataSet)` will likely show applications similar to the
historical use.

Data sets included in the HistData package are:

- [`Arbuthnot`](https://friendly.github.io/HistData/reference/Arbuthnot.md):

  Arbuthnot's data on male and female birth ratios in London from
  1629-1710

- [`Armada`](https://friendly.github.io/HistData/reference/Armada.md):

  The Spanish Armada

- [`Bowley`](https://friendly.github.io/HistData/reference/Bowley.md):

  Bowley's data on values of British and Irish trade, 1855-1899

- [`Breslau`](https://friendly.github.io/HistData/reference/Breslau.md):

  Halley's Breslau Life Table

- [`Cavendish`](https://friendly.github.io/HistData/reference/Cavendish.md):

  Cavendish's 1798 determinations of the density of the earth

- [`ChestSizes`](https://friendly.github.io/HistData/reference/ChestSizes.md):

  Quetelet's data on chest measurements of Scottish militiamen

- [`Cholera`](https://friendly.github.io/HistData/reference/Cholera.md):

  William Farr's Data on Cholera in London, 1849

- [`CholeraDeaths1849`](https://friendly.github.io/HistData/reference/CholeraDeaths1849.md):

  Daily Deaths from Cholera and Diarrhaea in England, 1849

- [`CushnyPeebles`](https://friendly.github.io/HistData/reference/CushnyPeebles.md):

  Cushny-Peebles data: Soporific effects of scopolamine derivatives

- [`Dactyl`](https://friendly.github.io/HistData/reference/Dactyl.md):

  Edgeworth's counts of dactyls in Virgil's Aeneid

- [`DrinksWages`](https://friendly.github.io/HistData/reference/DrinksWages.md):

  Elderton and Pearson's (1910) data on drinking and wages

- [`EdgeworthDeaths`](https://friendly.github.io/HistData/reference/EdgeworthDeaths.md):

  Edgeworth's Data on Death Rates in British Counties

- [`Fingerprints`](https://friendly.github.io/HistData/reference/Fingerprints.md):

  Waite's data on Patterns in Fingerprints

- [`Galton`](https://friendly.github.io/HistData/reference/Galton.md):

  Galton's data on the heights of parents and their children

- [`GaltonFamilies`](https://friendly.github.io/HistData/reference/GaltonFamilies.md):

  Galton's data on the heights of parents and their children, by family

- [`Guerry`](https://friendly.github.io/HistData/reference/Guerry.md):

  Data from A.-M. Guerry, "Essay on the Moral Statistics of France"

- [`HalleyLifeTable`](https://friendly.github.io/HistData/reference/HalleyLifeTable.md):

  Halley's Life Table

- [`Jevons`](https://friendly.github.io/HistData/reference/Jevons.md):

  W. Stanley Jevons' data on numerical discrimination

- [`Langren`](https://friendly.github.io/HistData/reference/Langren.md):

  van Langren's data on longitude distance between Toledo and Rome

- [`Macdonell`](https://friendly.github.io/HistData/reference/Macdonell.md):

  Macdonell's data on height and finger length of criminals, used by
  Gosset (1908)

- [`Mayer`](https://friendly.github.io/HistData/reference/Mayer.md):

  Mayer's data on the libration of the moon

- [`Michelson`](https://friendly.github.io/HistData/reference/Michelson.md):

  Michelson's 1879 determinations of the velocity of light

- [`Minard`](https://friendly.github.io/HistData/reference/Minard.md):

  Data from Minard's famous graphic map of Napoleon's march on Moscow

- [`Nightingale`](https://friendly.github.io/HistData/reference/Nightingale.md):

  Florence Nightingale's data on deaths from various causes in the
  Crimean War

- [`OldMaps`](https://friendly.github.io/HistData/reference/OldMaps.md):

  Latitudes and Longitudes of 39 Points in 11 Old Maps

- [`PearsonLee`](https://friendly.github.io/HistData/reference/PearsonLee.md):

  Pearson and Lee's 1896 data on the heights of parents and children
  classified by gender

- [`PolioTrials`](https://friendly.github.io/HistData/reference/PolioTrials.md):

  Polio Field Trials Data on the Salk vaccine

- [`Pollen`](https://friendly.github.io/HistData/reference/Pollen.md):

  5D dataset from the 1986 JSM Challenge

- [`Prostitutes`](https://friendly.github.io/HistData/reference/Prostitutes.md):

  Parent-Duchatelet's time-series data on the number of prostitutes in
  Paris

- [`Pyx`](https://friendly.github.io/HistData/reference/Pyx.md):

  Trial of the Pyx

- [`Quarrels`](https://friendly.github.io/HistData/reference/Quarrels.md):

  Statistics of Deadly Quarrels

- [`Saturn`](https://friendly.github.io/HistData/reference/Saturn.md):

  Laplace's Saturn data

- [`Snow`](https://friendly.github.io/HistData/reference/Snow.md):

  John Snow's map and data on the 1854 London Cholera outbreak

- [`Virginis`](https://friendly.github.io/HistData/reference/Virginis.md):

  J. F. W. Herschel's data on the orbit of the twin star gamma Virginis

- [`Wheat`](https://friendly.github.io/HistData/reference/Wheat.md):

  Playfair's data on wages and the price of wheat

- [`Yeast`](https://friendly.github.io/HistData/reference/Yeast.md):

  Student's (1906) Yeast Cell Counts

- [`ZeaMays`](https://friendly.github.io/HistData/reference/ZeaMays.md):

  Darwin's Heights of Cross- and Self-fertilized Zea May Pairs

Other packages containing data sets of historical interest include:

The
[`Guerry-package`](https://rdrr.io/pkg/Guerry/man/Guerry-package.html),
containing maps and other data sets related to Guerry's (1833) *Moral
Statistics of France*.

`morsecodes` from the (defunct) xgobi package for data from Rothkopf
(1957) on errors in learning Morse code, a classical example for MDS.

The psych package, containing Galton's `peas` data. The same data set is
contained in alr4 as
[`galtonpeas`](https://rdrr.io/pkg/alr4/man/galtonpeas.html).

The agridat contains a large number of data sets of agricultural data,
including some extra data sets related to the classical barley data
([`immer`](https://rdrr.io/pkg/MASS/man/immer.html) and
[`barley`](https://rdrr.io/pkg/lattice/man/barley.html)) from Immer
(1934):
[`minnesota.barley.yield`](https://kwstat.github.io/agridat/reference/minnesota.barley.yield.html),
[`minnesota.barley.weather`](https://kwstat.github.io/agridat/reference/minnesota.barley.weather.html).
